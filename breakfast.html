The Fruit Salad Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Mixed Fruits
  • Honey
  • Lemon
  • Fresh Mint Leaves

Preparation

                
                1. Wash and Prepare Fruits: Wash and peel (if necessary) the fruits, then cut them into bite-sized pieces. Remove any seeds or pits.

                2. Combine Fruits: Place all the prepared fruits in a large mixing bowl.

                3. Drizzle with Honey and Lemon Juice: Drizzle honey and lemon juice over the mixed fruits.

                4. Gently Toss: Gently toss the fruits to coat them evenly with the honey and lemon juice. Be careful not to mash the delicate fruits.

                5. Garnish and Serve: Garnish the fruit salad with fresh mint leaves for extra flavor and presentation. Serve immediately as a refreshing and healthy 
                snack.

The Chapathi Roll Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Chapathi
  • Cooked and seasoned filling
  • Sauce or chutney
  • Fresh vegetables
  • Cooking oil or ghee for pan-frying

Preparation

                
            1. Roll out chapathi dough into thin rounds and cook them on a hot griddle until they puff up and turn lightly golden.

            2. In a separate pan, sauté your choice of filling with spices and seasonings until fully cooked and flavorful.
            
            3. Place a chapathi on a flat surface and spread your sauce or chutney of choice over it.

            4. Add the cooked filling and top with fresh vegetables.

            5. Roll up the chapathi tightly, securing the filling inside, and serve hot as a delicious Chapathi Roll.

The Poori Masal Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Onions
  • Green Chilies
  • Spices
  • Salt and Water

Preparation

                
            1. Boil and peel the potatoes, then roughly mash them.

            2. In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves, allowing them to splutter.

            3. Add finely chopped onions and green chilies, sauté until the onions turn translucent.

            4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala, followed by the mashed potatoes. Cook for a few minutes, stirring well.

            5. Season with salt, sprinkle water, and let it simmer until the masala is cooked and aromatic. Serve hot with freshly fried pooris.

The Half Boil Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Toast
  • Butter

Preparation

                
            1. Boil Water: Fill a saucepan with enough water to cover the eggs you plan to cook. Add a pinch of salt to the water if desired, as it can help prevent
            the eggs from cracking.

            2. Bring Water to a Boil: Place the saucepan on the stove over medium-high heat. Bring the water to a rolling boil.

            3. Add Eggs: Gently lower the eggs into the boiling water using a slotted spoon or tongs. Be careful not to crack the eggs when placing them into the 
            water.

            4. Cook for the Desired Time: For a classic half-boiled egg with a runny yolk and set egg white, cook the eggs for about 4-6 minutes, depending on your 
            preference. Adjust the cooking time slightly if you prefer a firmer or more runny yolk.

            5. Remove and Serve: Using the slotted spoon or tongs, carefully remove the eggs from the boiling water and place them in egg cups or small bowls.
            You can 
            also serve them directly in their shells.

            6. To eat, tap the top of the eggshell with a knife or egg topper to create a small opening. Remove the top part of the eggshell, and you'll have a perfect 
            half-boiled egg.

            7. Serve the eggs with buttered toast for dipping and enjoy!

The Idli Sambar Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Split Pigeon Peas(Toor Dal)
  • Tamarind
  • Vegetables(tomatoes, carrots, drumsticks)
  • Sambar Powder
  • Mustard Seeds, Curry Leaves
  • Asafoetida

Preparation

                
            1. Cook Toor Dal: Wash the toor dal and pressure cook it with 2 cups of water until it's soft and mushy. Mash it well and set it aside.

            2. Prepare Tamarind Extract: Squeeze the soaked tamarind to extract its juice. Strain the juice and discard the solids.

            3. Cook Vegetables: In a separate pot, cook the chopped vegetables with enough water until they are tender. Add salt and sambar powder during 
            the last few minutes of cooking.

            4. Combine Dal and Vegetables: Add the cooked dal to the cooked vegetables and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed. 
            Bring it to a gentle boil.

            5. Tempering: In a separate small pan, heat oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add cumin seeds, dried red chilies, curry
            leaves, and a pinch of asafoetida. Pour this tempering over the prepared sambar and stir well.

The Sanveg Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Mixed Vegetables
  • Spices
  • Onions and Tomatoes
  • Oil or Ghee
  • Fresh Herbs

Preparation

                
            1. Preparation: Wash and chop the mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Dice the onions and tomatoes. Chop the fresh coriander leaves for 
            garnish.

            2. Sauté: Heat oil or ghee in a pan or pot. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Allow them to splutter. Then, add the chopped onions and sauté 
            until they become translucent.

            3. Spices: Add the spices - turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Sauté for a minute to release their flavors.

            4. Vegetables: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften. Then, add the mixed vegetables and stir well. Cover the pot and let the 
            vegetables cook on low heat until they are tender. You can add a little water if needed.

            5. Finishing Touch: Once the vegetables are cooked, sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with rice or 
            roti (Indian flatbread).
