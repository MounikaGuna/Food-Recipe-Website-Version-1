The Butter Chicken Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Tomato Sauce or Puree
  • Butter
  • Cream
  • Spices

Preparation

                
                1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, combine the yogurt, half of the minced garlic, half of the minced ginger, garam masala, chili powder, turmeric, 
                cumin, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken pieces and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or longer for better flavor).

                2. Cook the chicken: Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are
                are browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the cooked chicken from the pan and set it aside.

                3. Prepare the sauce: In the same pan, add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the remaining minced garlic  
                and ginger and cook for another 1-2 minutes. Stir in the tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes and cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

                4. Finish the dish: Return the cooked chicken to the pan and stir it into the sauce. Add the butter and heavy cream, stirring well until the sauce will
                become creamy and heated through. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes to let the flavors meld. Adjust salt, pepper, and chili powder to taste.

                5. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves, if desired. Serve your homemade butter chicken hot, ideally with naan, rice, or roti. Enjoy!

The Panner Tikka Masala Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Paneer
  • Marinade
  • Tomato-Based Sauce
  • Oil/Ghee
  • Fresh Coriander

Preparation

                
            1. Marinate the Paneer: Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt to create a marinade. Coat the paneer 
            cubes with this mixture and let them marinate for at least 30 minutes.

            2. Grill or Roast the Paneer: You can grill the marinated paneer cubes in an oven or on a stovetop griddle until they are slightly charred and cooked  
            through. This gives them a smoky flavor.

            3. Prepare the Sauce: In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee. Add cumin seeds and sauté onions until they turn translucent. Add ginger, garlic, and
            spices (coriander, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala) and cook until the spices release their aroma. Then, add chopped tomatoes and cook 
            until they soften.

            4. Blend and Simmer: Let the tomato mixture cool slightly, then blend it into a smooth paste. Return it to the pan, add cream, and simmer the sauce until
            it thickens and the oil starts to separate.

            5. Combine and Serve: Add the grilled paneer to the sauce, simmer for a few more minutes, and garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Your paneer tikka
            masala is ready to be served with naan, rice, or roti.

The Aloo Gobi Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Potatoes (Aloo)
  • Cauliflower (Gobi)
  • Cooking oil or ghee
  • Cumin seeds (Jeera)
  • Spices

Preparation

                
            1. Preparation: Wash and cut the cauliflower into bite-sized florets.Peel and dice the potatoes into small pieces.Heat oil or ghee in a pan.

            2. Tempering: Add cumin seeds to the hot oil and let them splutter.

            3. Cooking: Add the diced potatoes and cauliflower florets to the pan.Sprinkle with turmeric, cumin powder, coriander powder, and red chili powder.
            Mix well and sauté for a few minutes until the vegetables are coated with the spices.

            4. Cover and Simmer: Cover the pan and let the aloo gobi cook on low to medium heat.Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and burning.Cook until 
            the potatoes and cauliflower are tender. It may take around 15-20 minutes.

            5. Finishing Touch: Once the vegetables are cooked, sprinkle with garam masala for added flavor.Adjust salt and spices to taste.

            6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.

            7. Serve hot with rice or Indian bread like roti or naan.

The Rogan Josh Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Spices
  • Yogurt
  • Tomatoes
  • Oil or Ghee

Preparation

                
            1. Marinate the Meat: Cut the meat into bite-sized pieces and marinate it with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and Kashmiri red chili powder. Let it sit for 
            at least 30 minutes or longer in the refrigerator.

            2. Sauté the Spices: Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan or Dutch oven. Add whole spices like cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and bay leaves. Sauté for 
            a minute until they release their aroma.

            3. Add the Meat: Add the marinated meat to the pan and sear it until it turns brown on all sides. This helps lock in the flavors.

            4. Cook with Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and continue to cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the masala.

            5. Simmer and Serve: Add water to achieve your desired consistency and simmer the curry until the meat is tender and cooked through. Adjust the seasoning 
            with salt and more Kashmiri red chili powder if needed. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or naan bread.

The Tandoori Chicken Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • Yogurt(plain, unsweetened)
  • Tandoori masala spice blend
  • Garlic and ginger paste
  • Lemon juice for marinating and flavor

Preparation

                
            1. Marinate: In a large bowl, mix yogurt, tandoori masala spice blend, garlic and ginger paste, and lemon juice to create a marinade. Adjust the 
            amount of spice blend to your desired level of heat.

            2. Score the Chicken: Use a sharp knife to make 2-3 deep cuts in each chicken piece. This helps the marinade penetrate and flavor the meat.

            3. Marinate the Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, making sure they are well-coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least
            2 hours, or preferably overnight for better flavor.

            4. Preheat the Grill or Oven: If using a grill, preheat it to medium-high heat. If using an oven, preheat it to the highest broil setting.

            5. Cook the Chicken: Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers (if grilling) or place them on a baking sheet (if using the oven). Grill 
            or broil the chicken for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a nice charred appearance.

The Dal Makhani Recipe

Click to Watch Recipe Video

Ingredients

  • 1cup of Black Lentils (Urad Dal)
  • 1/4cup of Kidney Beans (Rajma)
  • 2-3 tablespoons of Butter or Ghee
  • 1, finely chopped Onion
  • 2, pureed Tomatoes

Preparation

        
            1. Soak Lentils and Kidney Beans: Wash and soak the black lentils and kidney beans separately in water for at least 6-8 hours or overnight.

            2. Boil Lentils and Kidney Beans: Drain the soaked lentils and kidney beans. In a large pot, add them along with enough water to cover and simmer 
            until they are soft and fully cooked. This can take around 30-40 minutes. Drain and set aside.

            3. Prepare the Base: In a separate pan, melt butter or ghee. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add tomato puree and cook 
            until the oil starts to separate from the mixture. You can also add ginger-garlic paste and spices like red chili powder, turmeric, and garam masala
            at this stage for added flavor.

            4. Add Cooked Lentils and Kidney Beans: Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the tomato-onion mixture. Mix well and simmer for about 15-20 
            min, allowing the flavors to meld. You may also add some cream for extra richness and creaminess.

            5. Serve: Garnish with fresh cream and fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, naan, or roti.
